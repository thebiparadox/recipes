Pumpkin Bread

Description

Pumpkin bread is an easy fall staple—with or without chocolate chips.

Ingredients

  • 219g all purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground all-spice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 150g dark brown sugar
  • 340g pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup canola oil
  • 1/4 milk or orange juice
  • 120g semi-sweet chocolate chips (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Grease a metal 9x5 loaf pan with non-stick spray. Set aside for now.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, all-spice, cloves, ginger, and salt together until combined. Set aside for now.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk eggs, granulated sugar, and brown sugar together until combined. Whisk in the pumpkin puree, oil, and milk or orange juice.
  4. Pour these wet ingridients into the dry ingredients and gently mix together using a rubber spatula or wooden spoon. There will be a few lumps: do not overmix.
  5. Gently fold in chocolate chips, if including.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan. Bake for 60-65 minutes, making sure to loosely cover the bread with aluminum foil halfway to prevent the top from getting too brown. The bread is down when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean with only a few crumbs.
  7. Allow bread to cool completely in the pan on a wirerack before removing and slicing.
  8. Cover and store leftover pumpkin bread at room temperature for up to 3-4 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Notes

  1. Make ahead tip: baked pumpkin break can be frozen for up to three months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and bring to room temperature before serving.
  2. Blot your pumpkin puree: whether canned or fresh, pumpkin puree has a lot of liquid content. In order to make a non-soggy loaf, measure your puree into a small bowl and blot with a paper towel gently to remove moisture before adding to batter.
